Some thoughts on Crypto and Gaming

August 24, 2022

I'm gonna preface this page by saying I'm not an economist, nor an active Crypto investor. I am not an expert on Cryptocurrency and there are many things I do not know about it. What I do know though is what the gaming community has been saying about NFT integration in games.

For those of you who have avoided this trend, NFTs (or Non-Fungible Tokens) are essentially a digital receipt that stores a token. A token can be many things: a photo, an executible, a video, whatever. This receipt verifies ownership of that token, and can be verified on the blockchain. These tokens are (allegedly) one-of-a-kind.

The thing about Crypto and NFTs as a whole is that they don't really appeal to anyone on the outside. And to those looking at the outside there are so many rug-pull and take the money and run scams that you can't easily tell a legitimate NFT business from a scam. For some reason though, many companies of different ages and experience are trying to break NFTs into the gaming sphere. My problem stems from, at its core, Game Design as a practice revolves around making interactive experiences that appeal to as many people as possible and are as inclusive as possible.

NFTs, by contrast, are very exclusive by design. They are meant to be owned by one person. This automatically conflicts with the core value of Game Design. There is already a fundamental disagreement in philosophies.
